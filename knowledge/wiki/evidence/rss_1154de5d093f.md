## Claim Supported

Speech and language technologies offer valuable opportunities for supporting mental health assessment through objective and interpretable cues. We present a systematic feature-based analysis framework leveraging perceptually grounded acoustic and linguistic characteristics, including prosody, vocal quality, semantic coherence, syntactic structure, and sarcasm. Using statistical analysis and interpretable machine learning (XGBoost with SHAP and LIME), we examine associations between speech features and validated symptom measures of depression, anxiety, and ADHD.
