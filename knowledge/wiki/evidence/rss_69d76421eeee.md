## Claim Supported

Core knowledge about physical objects -- e.g., their permanency, spatial transformations, and interactions -- is one of the most fundamental building blocks of biological intelligence across humans and non-human animals. While AI techniques in certain domains (e.g. vision, NLP) have advanced dramatically in recent years, no current AI systems can yet match human abilities in flexibly applying core knowledge to solve novel tasks. We propose a new AI approach to core knowledge that combines 1) visual representations of core knowledge inspired by human mental imagery abilities, especially as observed in studies of neurodivergent individuals; with 2) tree-search-based program synthesis for flexibly combining core knowledge to form new reasoning strategies on the fly.
