## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document synthesizes findings from **30+ independent literature searches** across peer-reviewed databases, clinical trials, systematic reviews, and grey literature to construct a practical, actionable framework for activating ADHD strengths. The framework moves beyond deficit-based models to leverage the neurodiversity paradigm, integrating evidence from neuroscience, psychology, occupational therapy, nutrition, chronobiology, and behavioral economics.

**Key Principle:** ADHD is not a deficit of attention or capability—it is a **dysregulation of attention allocation** driven by an **interest-based nervous system** (Dodson, 2013). The framework below teaches you to work *with* this system rather than against it.

## PILLAR 1: FLOW STATE ENGINEERING — Triggering Hyperfocus on Demand

### Finding 1.1: The Four Hyperfocus Triggers (and the Missing One)

Claim: ADHD hyperfocus is triggered by four conditions: **Interest, Challenge, Novelty, and Urgency** (the INCUP framework). Importance is NOT a trigger.

Source: Dr. William Dodson / ADHD research community
URL: https://www.donefirst.com/blog/the-incup-secret-5-motivating-factors-for-adhders
Date: Ongoing clinical framework
Excerpt: "Neurotypicals function with an importance-based nervous system—attention is driven by outcomes, rewards, and consequences. People with ADHD have an interest-based nervous system. When one or more of INCUP factors are met, attention, focus, and motivation come much easier."
Confidence: **High**

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION:**
- Before starting any "boring but necessary" task, **artificially inject** at least one INCUP factor:
  - **Interest:** Connect the task to something you care about. Example: "This spreadsheet helps me fund my motorcycle trip."
  - **Challenge:** Gamify it. Set a timer and try to beat your previous speed.
  - **Novelty:** Change your environment, use a new tool, or work from a different location.
  - **Urgency:** Create artificial deadlines (e.g., tell someone you'll send it by 3 PM).
  - **Passion:** Work on the task during your highest-energy time of day.

---

### Finding 1.2: The Neurobiology of ADHD Flow States

Claim: During hyperfocus, ADHD brains show neural patterns similar to Csikszentmihalyi's flow states—but with less voluntary control over entry and exit. Dopamine flooding in the prefrontal cortex creates a feedback loop where engagement produces dopamine, which sustains engagement.

Source: Biological Psychiatry (2019); Garcia Pimenta et al. (2024, PMID 38039699)
URL: https://www.zalfol.com/blog/science/hyperfocus-adhd/
Date: 2024
Excerpt: "When a task delivers sufficient reward, attention is no longer routed through top-down regulatory systems at all. The reward signal sustains engagement directly, bypassing the executive function deficit that makes sustained attention so costly for ADHD brains under normal conditions."
Confidence: **High**

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION:**
- **The Dopamine Bridge Protocol:** For tasks that don't naturally trigger reward, create a "bridge" of micro-rewards:
  1. Break the task into 5-10 minute chunks
  2. Pair each chunk with a small dopamine hit (favorite music, tasty snack, movement break)
  3. Create visible progress markers (checklist, progress bar, completed pile)
  4. The key insight: The ADHD brain has **two attentional modes**—impaired executive mode and intact reward-driven mode. Your job is to shift tasks into the second mode.

---

### Finding 1.3: EEG Evidence for ADHD Hyperfocus

Claim: During hyperfocus states, ADHD patients show reduced alpha and beta levels in frontal lobes, increased delta/theta activity in frontal midline (reflecting intrinsic reward experience), and higher parietal activation than controls—suggesting enhanced attentional control during absorbing tasks.

Source: Sklar (2013) / PMC
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7851038/
Date: 2013
Excerpt: "These results supported the notion that impairments to attention may be context-specific in patients with ADHD. It is possible that patients with ADHD are not impaired when in a hyperfocus state and may even have enhanced attentional control."
Confidence: **Medium-High**

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION:**
- Protect your hyperfocus states aggressively:
  - Use "Do Not Disturb" mode on all devices
  - Communicate to others that you are in a focus block
  - Set a visual timer (not an auditory alarm) to avoid jarring exits
  - Schedule transition buffers (15-30 minutes) after hyperfocus sessions

## PILLAR 2: CREATIVITY AMPLIFICATION — Systematic Divergent Thinking Enhancement

### Finding 2.1: ADHD and Divergent Thinking

Claim: Individuals with **high self-reported ADHD symptoms** (subclinical) outperform non-ADHD individuals on divergent thinking tasks. Clinical ADHD patients do not consistently show this advantage. There may be a "dose-response" relationship where subclinical ADHD traits maximize creativity without the full disorder's impairments.

Source: Hoogman et al. (2020) / Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews
URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33035524/
Date: 2020
Excerpt: "Most studies find evidence for increased divergent thinking for those with high ADHD scores (subclinical) but not for those with the disorder (clinical). The rates of creative abilities/achievements were high among both clinical and subclinical groups."
Confidence: **High**

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION:**
- **Divergent Thinking Activation Protocol:**
  1. **Lower inhibition deliberately:** Use brainstorming rules (no judgment, quantity over quality, wild ideas welcome)
  2. **Increase sensory input:** Work in stimulating environments, use background music, surround yourself with diverse stimuli
  3. **Leverage dopamine:** Creative tasks naturally activate dopamine—structure your day to do creative work when medication/natural dopamine peaks
  4. **Capture ideas immediately:** The ADHD brain generates ideas faster than it can organize them. Use voice memos, quick notes, or mind maps.
  5. **Separate generation from evaluation:** Never judge ideas during the generation phase. Create a "parking lot" for ideas to evaluate later.

---

### Finding 2.2: Psychostimulants and Creativity

Claim: Contrary to popular belief, psychostimulants did **not** show an overall negative effect on creativity in the reviewed literature. However, effects may vary by task type and individual.

Source: Hoogman et al. (2020) / Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews
URL: https://repository.ubn.ru.nl/bitstream/handle/2066/227072/227072.pdf
Date: 2020
Excerpt: "Psychostimulants did not show the often heard negative effects on creative thinking, but most research designs were suboptimal for studying these effects."
Confidence: **Medium**

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION:**
- Track your own creativity-medication relationship:
  - Rate creative output on and off medication
  - Some find medication helps with creative *execution* (finishing projects) even if it slightly dampens *ideation*
  - Consider "medication holidays" for pure brainstorming sessions if you notice decreased ideation

## PILLAR 4: SOCIAL SCAFFOLDING — Body Doubling and Parallel Work

### Finding 4.1: Body Doubling Science and Effectiveness

Claim: Body doubling (working alongside another person, either physically or virtually) shows consistent benefits for ADHD task initiation and sustained attention. 80% of ADHD clients reported significantly improved task completion when using body doubling. Focusmate grew from 1,000 to 500,000+ users between 2018-2024, with 70-85% reporting improved task initiation and 60-75% reporting 2-3x longer sustained focus.

Source: ADHD Coaching Association (2018); Focusmate User Survey (2023); Prolific Personalities Research
URL: https://www.cohorty.app/blog/body-doubling-for-adhd-virtual-co-working-that-actually-works
Date: Multiple
Excerpt: "Body doubling works best when the container is clear. Fewer decisions during the session lead to more follow-through. The presence of another person acts like an external anchor."
Confidence: **Medium-High** (strong convergent evidence, limited RCTs)

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION — Body Doubling Protocol:**
1. **Choose your double:** Someone steady, non-distracting, working on their own task
2. **Set the container:** Define the task, duration, and break structure BEFORE starting
3. **Minimize interaction:** The value is presence, not conversation
4. **Virtual options work:** Zoom/Focusmate with cameras on, microphones off
5. **AI doubles emerging:** A 2025 VR study found AI body doubles also improved sustained attention

---

### Finding 4.2: Social Facilitation Theory Underlies Body Doubling

Claim: Zajonc's (1965) classic social facilitation work and Bond & Titus (1983) meta-analysis confirm that mere presence of others enhances performance on well-learned tasks through increased arousal (mean d=0.43 for simple tasks). Accountability research shows commitment to another person increases task completion by 40-65% compared to private commitment.

Source: Prolific Personalities Research Paper
URL: https://prolificpersonalities.com/prolific-personalities-research-paper.pdf
Date: 2024
Excerpt: "Accountability research shows that commitment to another person increases task completion by 40-65% compared to private commitment. The mechanism operates through reputational concerns and social evaluation pressure."
Confidence: **High**

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION:**
- Create "accountability partnerships" for your most important goals:
  - Weekly check-in with a peer (15 minutes max)
  - Share specific commitments for the week
  - Report back on completion (no judgment, just accountability)
  - Consider professional ADHD coaching for structured accountability

## PILLAR 5: BIOLOGICAL OPTIMIZATION — Exercise, Nutrition, Sleep, Light

### Finding 5.1: Optimal Exercise Prescription for ADHD

Claim: Network meta-analysis reveals the optimal exercise prescription for inhibitory control in children/adolescents with ADHD: **moderate-intensity taekwondo, 70 minutes per session, twice weekly, for at least 20 weeks**. Alternative top options include table tennis and swimming. For general ADHD symptoms, therapeutic/alternative modalities (taekwondo, yoga) showed greater effects (SMD=0.45) than aerobic/endurance training (SMD=0.16).

Source: Chen et al. (2026) / Public Health; PMC12256947
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12256947/
Date: 2025-2026
Excerpt: "A moderate-intensity taekwondo exercise intervention model of 70 minutes twice a week for at least 20 weeks can achieve more satisfactory results. The 70-minute duration coincides with two critical periods of neurotransmitter release."
Confidence: **High**

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION:**
| Parameter | Optimal Setting | Why It Works |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| Type | Taekwondo > Table tennis > Swimming > Yoga | Combines motor learning, discipline, and moderate intensity |
| Duration | 70 minutes | First 60 min activate dopamine/norepinephrine release; next 10 min at peak concentration |
| Frequency | 2x/week | Sustainable adherence with strong effect |
| Period | ≥20 weeks | Brain plasticity requires sustained intervention |
| Intensity | Moderate | High intensity triggers cortisol that inhibits dopamine synthesis |

**For adults unable to do martial arts:** Any combination of: strength training + cardio + coordination exercises (dance, climbing, team sports) for 45-70 minutes, 2-3x/week.

### Finding 5.2: Nutrition and ADHD Cognitive Performance

Claim: Three nutritional interventions have evidence for ADHD symptom improvement: (1) Omega-3 fatty acid supplementation (EPA at ~2g/day shows efficacy), (2) Elimination diets for artificial food colorings and preservatives (category 4 evidence—"probably effective"), and (3) Addressing mineral deficiencies (zinc, magnesium, iron). Sugar restriction also reported anecdotally to enhance performance.

Source: Nordic Psychiatrist review; Sophia Kogan MD; multiple nutrition studies
URL: https://www.metiskurser.se/download/18.6fca4d6616e80065de41274/1576229272747/The%20Nordic%20Psychiatrist%20Issue%202%202019.pdf
Date: 2019
Excerpt: "Supplementation with omega-3 fatty acids is effective in ADHD treatment (category 5 evidence), as well as elimination diet, which has category 4 evidence—as probably effective."
Confidence: **Medium** (omega-3 has strongest evidence; elimination diets more variable)

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION:**
- **ADHD Nutrition Protocol:**
  1. **Protein at breakfast:** Stabilizes blood sugar and supports neurotransmitter production
  2. **Omega-3 supplementation:** EPA-rich fish oil, ~1-2g/day (consult physician)
  3. **Elimination trial:** Remove artificial colors, preservatives, and excess sugar for 3-4 weeks; reintroduce systematically to identify triggers
  4. **Address deficiencies:** Test and correct iron, zinc, magnesium if deficient
  5. **Mediterranean diet pattern:** Associated with lower ADHD symptoms in observational studies

### Finding 5.3: Sleep Optimization and Chronotherapy for ADHD

Claim: ADHD has strong associations with circadian rhythm dysfunction. Up to 80% of adults and 82% of children with ADHD have sleep disturbances. Delayed sleep-wake phase occurs in up to 78%. Melatonin (0.5mg for adults) advanced DLMO by 88 minutes and reduced ADHD symptoms by 14%. Bright light therapy (10,000 lux, morning) advanced DLMO by 31 minutes.

Source: Luu et al. (2025) / Frontiers in Psychiatry; van Andel et al. (2022)
URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41450833/
Date: 2025
Excerpt: "Melatonin and bright light therapy has advanced DLMO in both children and adults with ADHD. Emerging data correlate phase advancement with ADHD symptom improvement."
Confidence: **High**

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION — ADHD Sleep Restoration Protocol:**
1. **Fixed wake time:** Same time every day (most important anchor)
2. **Morning bright light:** 10-30 minutes of bright light within 30 minutes of waking (outdoor light preferred; 10,000 lux lamp in winter)
3. **Evening light restriction:** Blue light blocking glasses, dim lights, screen curfew 1-2 hours before bed
4. **Melatonin (if delayed phase confirmed):** Adults: 0.5mg, 4-6 hours before desired bedtime; Children: 3-6mg (consult pediatrician)
5. **Behavioral coaching addition:** Melatonin alone advanced biological clock but NOT sleep behavior—extensive behavioral coaching is necessary to translate DLMO advancement into actual sleep improvement
6. **Avoid late caffeine:** Stop caffeine 8+ hours before target bedtime

### Finding 5.4: White Noise for ADHD Focus

Claim: Auditory white noise (80 dB) improved cognitive task performance in ADHD children compared to stimulant medication in a pilot study. However, effects are **subgroup-dependent**: inattentive-dominant children benefit; hyperactive/impulsive-dominant children may perform WORSE with noise. The mechanism is stochastic resonance—moderate noise boosts weak neural signals in underaroused ADHD brains.

Source: Söderlund et al. (2016); PMC11585357; PMC9692615
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11585357/
Date: 2016-2024
Excerpt: "This pilot study shows that exposure to white noise resulted in a task improvement that was larger than the one with stimulant medication thus opening up the possibility of using auditory noise as an alternative, non-pharmacological treatment."
Confidence: **Medium-High** for inattentive type; **Medium** for combined type

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION:**
- Test white noise personally:
  - Inattentive-dominant: Try brown noise, white noise, or fan sounds during focus work
  - Hyperactive-dominant: May do better with silence or instrumental music
  - Use noise-canceling headphones in noisy environments
  - Volume matters: ~80 dB in studies, but comfort comes first

## PILLAR 6: COGNITIVE-EMOTIONAL TRAINING — Beyond Medication

### Finding 6.1: DBT Skills Training for ADHD

Claim: Online dialectical behavior therapy skills training (DBT-ST) significantly decreased global ADHD symptoms, inattention, hyperactivity, impulsivity (37.4% improvement), and emotion regulation difficulties (31.4% improvement) while increasing life satisfaction and functionality. In-person DBT showed medium-to-large effect sizes for depression and anxiety reduction.

Source: Ulusoy et al. (2025) / Psychotherapy Research; Frontiers Psychiatry
URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38359387/
Date: 2025
Excerpt: "DBT-ST was found effective against the participants' ADHD symptoms. The treatment was additionally found to improve their emotion regulation and quality of life."
Confidence: **High**

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION — DBT Skills for ADHD:**
The four DBT modules all apply directly to ADHD:
1. **Mindfulness:** Improves attention and focus (start with 5-minute guided practices)
2. **Distress Tolerance:** Manages impulsivity (TIPP skill: Temperature, Intense exercise, Paced breathing, Paired muscle relaxation)
3. **Emotion Regulation:** Controls emotional reactions (especially RSD—see below)
4. **Interpersonal Effectiveness:** Improves relationships strained by ADHD symptoms

---

### Finding 6.2: Adapted Mindfulness for ADHD

Claim: The Mindful Awareness Practices (MAPs) for ADHD Program showed significant improvements in self-reported inattentive, hyperactive-impulsive, depressive, and anxious symptoms. Significant improvements emerged on conflict detection and set-shifting. Improvements were sustained at 3-month follow-up. Retention was 78%.

Source: Zylowska et al. (2008, 2009) / PMC4403871
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4403871/
Date: 2008-2009
Excerpt: "Retention rates were high with the majority (78%) of participants completing the treatment. Participants reported significant practice outside of weekly sessions and overall high satisfaction with the treatment."
Confidence: **Medium-High** (promising but limited by small sample, no control group)

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION — ADHD-Adapted Mindfulness:**
- Traditional silent sitting meditation often fails for ADHD. Modified approaches work better:
  - **Movement meditation:** Walking meditation, yoga, tai chi
  - **Shorter sessions:** 3-5 minutes rather than 20-30
  - **Guided practices:** Use apps/videos to reduce self-direction demands
  - **S.T.O.P. mnemonic:** S=Stop, T=Take a breath, O=Observe, P=Proceed
  - **Body posture adjustment:** Use physical cues to shift mental states
  - **Imagery:** Visualize a mountain for stability during overwhelm

### Finding 6.3: Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD)

Claim: RSD is reported by approximately 77% of adults with ADHD in qualitative studies, yet is NOT included in DSM-5-TR criteria. RSD manifests as intense physical discomfort, masking (shaping behaviors for acceptance), withdrawal, loneliness, and disrupted social functioning.

Source: Ginapp et al. (2023); Rowney-Smith et al. (2026); Psychology Today
URL: https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/if-i-be-waspish/202604/rejection-sensitivity-dysphoria-the-actual-research
Date: 2026
Excerpt: "33 participants (about 77%) reported struggling with RSD even though RSD (and emotional dysregulation, more generally) is not included as a core symptom of ADHD in the DSM-5-TR."
Confidence: **Medium** (growing qualitative evidence; formal measures still being developed)

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION — RSD Management:**
1. **Name it:** Understanding that RSD is a neurological phenomenon, not a character flaw, reduces shame
2. **Pre-processing:** Before situations where rejection is possible, write out worst-case scenarios and plan responses
3. **Reality testing:** Ask trusted people "Did that actually happen the way I experienced it?"
4. **DBT distress tolerance:** TIPP skills for acute RSD episodes
5. **Medication discussion:** Some find alpha-agonists (guanfacine, clonidine) or MAOIs help with emotional dysregulation
6. **Avoid isolation:** Withdrawal worsens RSD—maintain connections even when it feels hard

| Skill | Technique | Application |
|-------|-----------|-------------|

## PILLAR 7: ENVIRONMENTAL DESIGN — Tools, Apps, and Sensory Supports

### Finding 7.1: Digital Interventions — Mixed Evidence

Claim: Systematic review of 26 systematic reviews (34,442 participants) found that digital interventions for ADHD show potential benefits but effectiveness remains **inconclusive due to low evidence quality**. Adverse effects (physical discomfort, emotional reactions, video game addiction) were reported in 30% of reviews. Psychoeducation digital interventions showed the strongest evidence.

Source: Powell et al. (2025) / BMC Psychiatry; QUB
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12016436/
Date: 2025
Excerpt: "While digital interventions for ADHD show potential benefits, their effectiveness remains inconclusive due to low evidence quality. Adverse effects, particularly from video games, have been reported but are inconsistently documented."
Confidence: **High** for the systematic review itself

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION — Technology Selection Framework:**
| Category | Evidence Level | Examples | Recommendation |
|----------|---------------|----------|----------------|
| Psychoeducation apps | Moderate | Various | Useful for learning |
| Computerized cognitive training | Low-Moderate | Cogmed, Brain Game | Limited transfer to real-world function |
| Gamified task managers | Low (but promising) | Forest, Focus ADHD, Focus To-Do | Worth trying; evidence building |
| VR interventions | Low | Various VR attention apps | Improved attentional vigilance in some studies |
| Body doubling platforms | Low (strong user evidence) | Focusmate, FLOWN, Cohorts | High user-reported effectiveness |
| Visual timers | Moderate | Time Timer | Strong ADHD-specific support |

### Finding 7.2: Visual Timers and Time Management

Claim: Visual timers (showing time as a disappearing disk) significantly improve self-regulation and time management for ADHD. A Florida Atlantic University study found Time Timer significantly improved self-regulation skills in children aged 2-4. Time-related interventions with occupational therapy support improved time perception deficits in children with ADHD aged 9-15.

Source: Time Timer research; PMC5852175
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5852175/
Date: 2017
Excerpt: "The majority of the TADs were for compensating for deficits in time perception or time orientation. An example of a product compensating for deficits in time perception was a visual timer."
Confidence: **Medium-High**

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION:**
- **The Time Timer Protocol for ADHD:**
  1. Use visual timers for EVERY task transition (not just work—also breaks, getting ready, cooking)
  2. Start with short intervals (10-15 minutes) and build up
  3. Pair with the Pomodoro Technique: work block + break block
  4. For hyperfocus protection: Set a visual timer as a gentle exit cue rather than an alarm
  5. Place timer in peripheral vision so time awareness is ambient, not effortful

### Finding 7.3: Fidget Tools

Claim: Tactile stimulation devices (fidgets) show promising evidence for increasing on-task behavior in ADHD. A 2022 single-case design found fidget spinners produced "large immediate and sustained increases in on-task behavior." Stress ball use reduced distractions and improved written expression scores. However, effects vary by individual and fidget type—quiet, unobtrusive fidgets work best.

Source: PMC9120292; Stalvey & Brasell (2006); ERIC EJ1272882
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9120292/
Date: 2022
Excerpt: "Visual analysis of time-series graphs showed large immediate and sustained increases in on-task behavior during fidget spinner use."
Confidence: **Medium** (promising but small studies, mixed results)

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION:**
- Choose quiet, unobtrusive fidgets:
  - Squeeze balls, fidget cubes, Silly Putty, worry stones
  - Avoid visually distracting fidgets (spinners) in shared spaces
  - Chewing gum is also an effective oral fidget
  - Match fidget to task: tactile for reading/listening, movement for thinking/creating

Claim: Adults with ADHD thrive in careers aligned with creativity, high energy, rapid problem-solving, and hyperfocus capacity. Some folks like a quiet job that doesn't require much brainpower so they can let their minds wander through the day. But I'd say the majority of us like jobs that include a level of self-direction and quite a bit of novelty."

**ACTIONABLE RECOMMENDATION — ADHD Career Fit Assessment:**
| Your Strength | Matching Career Clusters |
|--------------|--------------------------|

## CRITICAL INSIGHTS FROM THE LITERATURE

1. **ADHD is not a deficit of attention—it's a dysregulation of attention allocation.** The same brain that can't focus on a spreadsheet can hyperfocus for 8 hours on a passion project.

2. **Importance does not motivate the ADHD brain.** Interest, challenge, novelty, urgency, and passion do. Stop trying to "should" yourself into productivity.

3. **Hyperfocus is not a gift layered on top of ADHD—it IS the dopamine dysregulation expressing itself as absorption.** Learn to trigger it, protect it, and exit it gracefully.

4. **External scaffolding is not cheating—it's compensation.** Visual timers, body doubling, written lists, and visual schedules are prosthetics for a working memory difference.

5. **Medication helps symptoms; skills training helps life.** The MTA study showed combination treatment (medication + behavioral) outperformed either alone for functional outcomes.

6. **Circadian rhythm dysfunction is a core ADHD phenotype, not a side effect.** Treating sleep with chronotherapy (light, melatonin, routine) improves ADHD symptoms directly.

7. **Strengths-use predicts wellbeing more than symptom severity.** The most successful ADHD adults are not those with the fewest symptoms—they're those who best match their environment to their strengths.

8. **Digital tools show promise but lack rigorous evidence.** Be an informed consumer—test tools personally, track outcomes, and don't abandon proven strategies for shiny apps.

9. **White noise helps inattentive-dominant ADHD but may harm hyperactive-dominant.** Personalize sensory interventions based on your subtype.

10. **Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria affects ~77% of adults with ADHD** but is invisible in diagnostic criteria. Naming it and using DBT distress tolerance skills changes lives.
